# Game Director Audit (Current Build)

## Goal
Create a production-style audit that identifies:
- missing life-sim pillars
- rough/underdeveloped systems
- immediate high-leverage fixes

Scoring scale: **1 (weak) → 10 (excellent)**.

## System Scorecard

| Pillar | Depth | Coherence | Replayability | UX Clarity | Balance Risk | Notes |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---|
| Core life loop (age-up) | 7 | 6 | 7 | 7 | 6 | Strong breadth; historically prone to stacked pressure interactions. |
| Relationships | 6 | 5 | 7 | 7 | 5 | Good event surface; low long-memory and trust-state persistence. |

## Missing / Thin Areas (Highest Priority)

1. **Trait-driven identity simulation**
   - Need persistent values/temperament that shape every major outcome stream.

2. **Relationship memory model**
   - Add trust, resentment, promises-kept, conflict history, and reconciliation arcs.

3. **Employee career depth parity**
   - Expand managerial politics, specialization tracks, and mid-career pivots.

4. **Mental health continuum**
   - Move beyond stress to anxiety/depression/burnout treatment and recovery routes.

5. **Late-life gameplay**
   - Retirement identity, health decline decisions, legacy transfer, family responsibilities.

## Existing Rough Spots

- **Balance coupling:** many systems independently alter stress/fame/money; needs a unifying director layer.
- **Pacing mismatch:** some career paths are deep and multi-layered; others remain single-click dominant.
- **Consequence memory:** decisions can feel local and short-lived rather than systemic and compounding.

## Roadmap (Studio Style)

### Phase 1 (now)
- Add a **Director Pass** for annual pressure/recovery balancing and telemetry.
- Surface pressure/recovery summary to player for transparency.

### Phase 2
- Relationship memory and trust stack (friend/lover/family).
- Employee-career overhaul to match founder depth.

### Phase 3
- Mental health treatment pathways and long-horizon effects.
- Late-life and retirement expansion with legacy mechanics.

## Success Metrics

- Reduced stress runaway frequency in 20+ year simulations.
- Lower probability of one subsystem overwhelming all others.
- Better readability of yearly outcomes via telemetry and summary lines.
- Stronger parity between career archetypes (employee/founder/creator/athlete).
